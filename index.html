Animals We Care For

Each habitat is designed to support animal wellbeing while giving visitors meaningful learning moments about empathy and responsible care.

Rabbit

Gentle companions for safe feeding and handling practice.

Tortoise

Long-living residents that teach patience and habitat stewardship.

Hedgehog

Nocturnal friends introducing biodiversity and respectful observation.

Parrots

Intelligent birds that encourage enrichment-based interaction.

Koi Fish

Pond life examples of water quality, balance, and routine care.

Guinea Pig

Social small mammals ideal for beginner animal-care education.

Cats

Friendly farm companions that add warmth to the garden environment.

Vegetables, Bees & Hydroponics

We combine traditional growing with modern techniques so visitors can compare methods, outcomes, and sustainability benefits in one place.

Vegetable Garden

  • Leafy greens, herbs, and local crops for seasonal harvesting and direct sales.
  • Learning on composting, soil health, and organic methods.
  • Community harvest days connecting food and wellbeing.

Bees & Honey Harvest

  • We rear bees carefully to support pollination and ecosystem health.
  • Seasonal honey harvest activities are part of our educational sessions.
  • Fresh Tempua Kebun honey is available for sale alongside our vegetables.

Hydroponic System

  • Vertical: stacked channels that maximize small footprints.
  • Horizontal: easy-access rows for routine maintenance.
  • Water-efficient, clean growing for urban-friendly farming.
